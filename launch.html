Launch: Personal Statement

If you're applying to law school, chances are you need to write a few essays for your application. First and foremost is the personal statement. But what is the personal statement? How should you write it? What topic should you choose? What does the admissions committee want from you?

Stop. Take a deep breath. We're here to help.

Launch: Personal Statement is a self-guided online course that will walk you through the law school personal statement writing process.

Through six video classes with corresponding exercises, you'll not only learn what makes a great personal statement—you'll also draft your own.

Step by step, we'll examine what the personal statement really is, discover what schools are looking for, discuss the top concepts you should include, brainstorm for a personalized topic, generate the meat of your essay, and organize your prose into a coherent structure. By the end of the course, you'll have a personal statement draft targeted to your top schools.

Launch: Personal Statement is designed to let you work at your own pace, so you can take the time you need to balance your life with your application process. Do you need to watch class videos at two in the morning, or balance your notebook on your knee to write your essay on the train? No problem. Course material will be available on demand until August 31st, 2014.

If you don't know where to begin, and want detailed instruction on how to write a top-notch personal statement for law school, this course is for you.

Is this class for me?

You should take this class if:

  • You're unclear on the purpose and intent of the personal statement—what IS it?
  • You're confused or indecisive about what to include in your personal statement.
  • You don't have a clear idea of what schools want to see or how to give it to them.
  • You've never written a personal essay before.
  • You've tried to write a personal statement, but somehow it keeps coming out all wrong.
  • You know you need to write your personal statement, but you just can't get over the hump and put something down on paper.
  • You've done your research on the law job market, and are absolutely convinced that law is the career for you.
  • You have a lot on your plate and need a course that works with your schedule.
  • You want to attend law school in the United States or Canada.
  • You've just finished your junior or senior year of college and want to get a leg up on the competition.
  • You've taken a year after college to explore your options, and now know for sure that law is your goal.
  • You've been immersed in your career for five or ten years, and want to take positive steps to start making the change to law.

You should not take this class if:

  • You already have a personal statement draft or three in hand, and you just need to edit. Instead, check out our personal statement editing course, Refine: Personal Statement.
  • You aren't sure that a law career is the best choice for you.
  • You haven't finished your junior year of college—concentrate on getting great grades and experience instead!
  • You haven't done any research about the current law market.
  • You don't know which law schools you want to apply to.
  • You know exactly what you want to write about and how to go about writing your personal statement.
  • You're certain the personal statement topic you've chosen will impress the admissions committee of your choice.
